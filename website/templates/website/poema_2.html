Poema 2

The sea stirs silent in a ripple On meddled shore, still dream hung, sun worn, Ships outdone by a shadowed pall As they sit like giant ghosts on the horizon.

In the beckoning of our nets shrimps flick dismay invisibly, Fleet sand sparks leaving clouds of smoke To tell us that they’ve gone
